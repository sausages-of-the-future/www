Context

Government as a Platform is the civic infrastructure for the digital age. It's an huge endeavour we think is equivilent in size and effort to building the sewer system in the Victoria era, or the railways. We have assumed that building it is something that spans multiple parliaments and outlives the the people involved in this initial thinking.

We have assumed a certain inevitability to a system of government that is built on solid digital foundations. The Internet and the web are transforming our lives and people now demand simple, convenient, secure efficient, transparent and real-time services. Countries like Estonia are leading the way and other countries are following, including the UK. To date though we have been digitising an analogue state.

There is no right answer. There are parts of the model we're proposing that we know we don't know enough about. There is a risk of over confidence or over promise so we're aknowleding we have a lot to learn and we need to optimise for learning.

The challenge ahead does not lend itself to a big bang release. We cannot imagine a scenario where overnight one switches from a system of government built up over 160 years, with decades of out-dated, legacy systems, slow-to-change institutions more used to command and control to a world where the foundations of the state are totally different, be they, cultural, data, tech, privacy, security or operational. The only way to deliver this is iteratively, learning as we go.
