Policy

Policy still matters. You need a clear policy intent, policy rules, set by Ministers backed by legislation in Parliament

Majority of policy rules are set in the service layer. Most of the "Who can do what, when, and why" rules are enacted here.

I think there's a distinction betweentypes of policy we havent cracked yet. UC rules are clearly service layer: minister says X, single place to pay out the money based on the rules. But for selling property, eligibility for being registered as a legal driver the rules are probably at the registry layer? I think anyway. Maybe the difference between the reads and the writes? [just realised, this is all explained below ]

Not too different from today, so long as policy remains at the level of high level rules, rather than service design detail (the "How")

Policy should set the rules, don't design THE service. There could be many services, designed differently to follow same policy rules, seeking same policy intent

However...

Some important policy decisions now reside with registrars, notably when should the information in a register be updated? In what circumstances would an individual service we allowed to update the information in a register, given many services would rely on said register.

We can now support many, different services delivering the same policy intent.

Ministers could deliver policy intent via a portfolio of many services, since services much cheaper to build and run, with more measurable results.

"One set of policy rules; many different services following the rules". Overlaps OK, since data canonical.

Each service in a policy portfolio could be more focused, tailored and adaptable. Could try very different service design approaches, and compete with other services to deliver better outcomes. Inside public sector; or outside; or across both.

Services meeting same policy intent could be run by public, private or 3rd sector, all enacting same policy rules.

Proper experimentation now easier, because canonical data and agility

Management info transformed. More measureable outcomes, cross-service (with consent)

Randomised Control Trial's much easier due to service flexibility and better data

Show the democratic seams

Doing the hard work to make it more democratic (mission to explain who accountable for what et. Because Democracy)

Beware unintended consequences (e.g. simple to create 100 x companies... is that OK?)
