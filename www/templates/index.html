Government as a platform

This is a prototype of how government as a platform could work

Services

Services are focused on user need, regardless of institutional boundaries, are quick to build and change.

They can be run by central and local government, and by 3rd sector and commercial organisations. Because making new services becomes cheap and quick, there is an ecosystem of overlapping services.

Small, interconnected services mean it is possible to interact with multiple layers of government and other organisations in a single transaction. Users taking proof across the boundaries of a service using digital-proof.

Accountability for services is clear. Services are real-time.

Examples
  • Central government services
  • City government services
  • Non-profit
  • Commercial services

Common platforms

Shared platforms and software make it easy to build services.

Government helps maintain open-source code libraries, documentation and communities of people building on the platform.

Examples
  • Blogs
  • Identity verification
  • Notifications
  • Payments
  • Performance
  • Publishing
  • Search

Trust and consent

Services access user's data via a data trust and consent layer. This layer is vital to maintain data quality and privacy, and to prevent fraud.

Because a user has control and oversight over what data is used and for what purpose, it is possible to allow 3rd party organsiaitons to build on the platform.

Examples
  • Service permissions
  • User permissions

Registers

Registries are organisations that look after the canonical personal and open data required for the functioning of the country, in the public interest. These datasets are called registers.

Registries and registers are long lived, hard to create, hard to change and hard to abolish.

  • List of registries
  • Example register
